- **Ordinary Courage**: True courage is about being vulnerable in everyday moments—such as admitting when we are lost or asking for help. Additionally, trying to protect ourselves from disappointment by downplaying exciting things only serves to minimize our joy when good things actually happen.
- **Compassion, Boundaries, and Accountability**:

- True compassion is a relationship between equals that requires us to be in touch with our own darkness and suffering.
- Compassion is deeply dependent on setting clear boundaries and holding people accountable. Blaming and shaming are not the same as accountability; they actually shift the focus away from the original behavior.
- Accountability means separating the person from their behavior. Failing to set boundaries leads to resentment, which makes practicing compassion impossible.
- **Connection**: Connection is the energy that exists when people feel seen, heard, and valued without judgment. The cultural myth of self-sufficiency is a major barrier to connection. To truly give with an open heart, we must also learn to receive help without judging ourselves.
